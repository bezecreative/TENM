NCS & College of Clean

The method is the differentiator. For the 7-Eleven car-wash menu, we used RealEye webcam eye-tracking to record exactly where attention landed across four design variations — and where "complexity-induced apathy" caused people to stop reading. In the legacy layout, post-test participants correctly identified top-package features only 40% of the time. The design was failing to communicate value, and no amount of polish would have revealed that without the data.

We rebuilt around "information blocking" and F-pattern reading flows — separating titles, details, and prices into distinct visual blocks and exaggerating hierarchy on the items that mattered most. The result: users began reading the right content almost immediately on screen load. We turned a confusing interface into one that guides people to what they need.

"Polish can't fix a path people can't find."

What this means for TENM

A coalition platform is a high-content environment — a curriculum hub, Blueprint for Change resources, podcast episodes, policy and platform agendas, news, and a directory of endorsing organizations across your five issue pillars. The risk is the same one the 7-Eleven study exposed: a site can look finished and still leave families and educators unable to find what they came for. Our research-first phase — heatmap tracking, screen testing, and interviews with actual community members — maps how the public really moves through your information before a single page is designed. That's how we shorten the path to the curriculum hub, reduce friction for advocates, and cut the volume of questions your staff fields after launch.

It's also why the discovery phase comes first in the timeline. The sitemap, curriculum-hub structure, and visual direction are all built on evidence and approved by your team before development begins — so the platform is intuitive on day one, not after a year of guesswork.

03 — Technical Scope

Everything a coalition
platform must do.

Engineered to a professional standard, built for non-technical staff to run, and designed to connect cleanly with the systems your coalition already uses.

A1 · Platform
Native WordPress Build
We build new functionality natively into your existing WordPress environment and theme — preserving your current design standards, logos, brand colors, and typography. A modern front end for your audiences and a backend your non-technical staff can manage independently after launch.
A2 · Curriculum Hub
Category-Searchable Curriculum Hub
A custom, category-based infrastructure with dynamic taxonomy filtering and a search-suggestions dropdown that surfaces resources as users type — supporting external links and downloadable files, optimized for low-bandwidth regions. A localized map lets users trace the regional footprint of specific curriculum resources.
A3 · Accessibility
ADA & WCAG 2.1 AA
Compliance is built into the workflow, not bolted on. We audit accessibility throughout design and development, document conformance before launch, and hand off tools and support so staff hold the standard as new content is published.
A4 · Analytics
Dual-Layer Tracking & Reporting
Google Analytics and Microsoft Clarity integrated natively into the WordPress framework — pairing quantitative traffic data with visual behavioral insight. Your team monitors traffic growth, retention, and curriculum downloads through automated dashboards without leaving the backend.
A5 · Directory & Portal
Partners, Events & Integrations
A template-driven directory for ~25 coalition partners — each with photos and social links — plus a secure login portal for roughly 25 partner logins. An event system handles RSVPs and post-event recordings, your MailChimp is preserved, and a secure donation platform is connected.
A6 · Training
Staff Independence
Recorded, hands-on training sessions — general use, curriculum-hub management, and analytics dashboards — plus a step-by-step how-to video library. Your team manages the platform confidently, regardless of future personnel changes.
